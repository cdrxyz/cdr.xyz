## Overview

A family-run jewellery shop had built a loyal local following over years of craft and care — but behind the display cases, the owner was running an invisible second shift every night. Bookkeeping in QuickBooks. Inventory counts scribbled on notepads. Instagram posts composed after hours. Customer follow-up emails that kept slipping through the cracks.

The business was healthy, but the owner was burning out. She needed automation that didn't require a technical team to set up or maintain — something that could slot into her existing tools and workflows without disruption.

Cedar Labs deployed OpenClaw, our open-source automation platform, configured specifically for the shop's day-to-day operations. Over two months, we built and tuned a set of automations that now handle the repetitive back-office work that used to consume evenings and weekends.

The deployment covered four core areas:

- **Accounting**: OpenClaw connects to QuickBooks Online to automatically categorize transactions, reconcile bank feeds, and generate weekly profit-and-loss snapshots — eliminating hours of manual bookkeeping each week
- **Sales & Inventory**: New sales are logged from the point-of-sale system into a tracking sheet, and OpenClaw monitors stock levels to flag items approaching reorder thresholds, sending the owner a summary every Monday morning
- **Marketing**: Social media posts are drafted from recent product photos and inventory highlights, queued in a review buffer, and published on a consistent weekly schedule — turning an erratic social presence into a reliable marketing rhythm
- **Customer Operations**: Automated follow-up emails for repair pickups, custom order status updates, and post-purchase thank-you notes keep customers informed without the owner writing every message manually

All automations run on the shop's existing laptop, with no cloud infrastructure required. OpenClaw's local-first architecture meant zero ongoing hosting costs and no data leaving the shop's premises.

The owner now spends her evenings at home with her family instead of reconciling QuickBooks, and her weekends free of social media scheduling. The shop's Instagram following grew 40% in the first month of consistent posting, and customer feedback scores improved as response times dropped from days to hours.
